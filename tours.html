Cancún,Riviera Maya & Yucatan Península

Quintana Roo state, on Yucatán Península. Cancún is located on the Yucatan Channel that separates Mexico from the island of Cuba in the Greater Antilles. The Cancún area is also known as the Mexican Caribbean.

The 120 Km (75 miles) coastline from Puerto Morelos to Tulum, considered the Riviera Maya, includes magical places as Playa Del Carmen, Puerto Aventuras and Akumal. The Riviera Maya has become one of the most favorite destinations in Mexico offering different activities such as golfing, fishing, natural parks,archeological sites, jungle expeditions, ecotourism, adventure tourism and one of most beautiful beaches in the world.

Playa Del Carmen Located 50 minutes from the Cancun airport is half way between Cancun and Tulum at the center of Riviera Maya. Playa del Carmen is one of the most popular area to visit, especially Fifth Avenue. Here you may find many lounges bars restaurants, hotels, and clubs. Enjoy drinks, music and meet many interesting and beautiful people.

Cobá

Quintana Roo México

Located in land 42 KM (26 miles), North West of Tulum, Coba was re-discovered in 1926 by Lord Erick S. Thompson. In Coba you will find the highest pyramid in all of the Yucatan Peninsula with 42 meters high and 122 steps. It shows in the construction of its buildings a strong influence of the Mayas of Central America.

Xel-Ha

Quintana Roo México

The aquatic sanctuary of Xel-Ha fed simultaneously by subterranean freshwater springs and mixed with Caribbean seawater, is reserved for breeding countless species of colorful tropical fish. Suntan lotions and chemical products are not allowed in the area to protect the sensitive environment. The lagoon has attractive walkways that wind through the surrounding jungle.

Quintana Roo México

Located 131 KM (81 miles) south of Cancun along the coast of the Mexican Caribbean, this archaeological site was built in the post classic period. It was originally called Zama which means Sunrise in the Mayan language. From the many findings at the site, it has been proven that other cultures had traded with the Mayas.
